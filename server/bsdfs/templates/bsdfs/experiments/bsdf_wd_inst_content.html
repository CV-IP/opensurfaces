Your task is to adjust controls to make a blob look like it is made out of the same material as another shape. When you are done, the blob and the target shape should look similar.

You will be given the controls on the right to edit the blob. Please try moving the sliders around to see what happens to the blob in the middle. If you have trouble finding the color, you can click on the "target" image to set the color.

If you cannot complete the task (e.g. the target has more than one material), click "I give up". Please give a reason for why you cannot match the blob to the target. If you give up, it will count as a completed task.

Please scroll down for examples.


Description of the controls

Reflection Type
metals often have colored reflections; most other things have uncolored reflections
Distinctness of Image
the sharpness/blurriness of the reflection
Contrast
the difference between the bright and dark regions
Color
the average color of the blob; you can also set this by clicking on the target image
Brightness
whether the blob is bright or dark

Note: Please note that the most important part to get right is the "Distinctness of Image" and "Contrast" controls.

What if the target is textured?

If the target has a texture, please try and find the closest match -- we understand that this is a subjective task.


Why are we doing this?

We are building a large database of objects and materials from images on the web, and we want to categorize them by metrics such as roughness and apparent contrast. You will be giving us these metrics as you complete the task.

This is part of ongoing research at Cornell University. Thank you for participating!
